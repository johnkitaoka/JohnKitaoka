About

          My name is John Kitaoka, and I'm a second-year undergraduate student from Rochester, Minnesota. I'm working towards a double major in Accounting and Computer Science, with a cumulative GPA of 4.0/4.0 and expected graduation date of May 2021. I've worked with all sorts of technologies and languages, from writing statistics-laden LaTeX reports, to training generations of artificial genomes to play a popular mobile game, to learning CSS and JavaScript through building this website.

          Apart from my studies, I play water polo for the University of Wisconsin men's team, I participate in hackathons, and I am a member of UW-Madison's Capital Management Club and the Association for Computing Machinery.

          While most comfortable in Python and Java, I'm also very familiar with SQL, HTML, CSS, and Visual Basic. Last summer, I was a data analytics intern at Geotek, Inc., where I decreased total data storage per year by 21%, and saved the company thousands of dollars in third-party software expenses by creating an accessible expense-calculating algorithm. I also started working in the Data Science labs in the University of Wisconsin, Madison, as a data science research assistant to analyze traffic engineering data for the city of Madison. Currently, I am an undergraduate TA for CS301: Data Programming I in the UW-Madison Department of Computer Science.

VAC-Calculator Algorithm

While working at Geotek, Inc. I was given the responsibility of finding a new way of calculating labor and material expenses for the manufacturing department. I created a recursive binary search tree algorithm that not only reduced server traffic and storage consumption, it outperformed an independent third-party software, saving the company thousands of dollars. The algorithm was packaged in a Django web app and reflected accurate expense reports in real time.

SQL Auto-Graph GUI

From my experiences with database manipulation and statistical analysis in the Data Science labs at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, I developed a simple iteration of Microsoft Access - a straightforward database viewer with easy SQL query construction. As an added feature, a simple GUI allowed for graphical analysis and quick data visualization.

Neuro-Evolutionary Jetpack Joyride AI

For personal interests, I rewrote an existing Python 'Flappy Bird' open-source code to reflect the gameplay, sprites, and physics of 'Jetpack Joyride', and a genome-based evolutionary neural network, NEAT-Python, was then applied to the game. Using a tanh-based fitness function, genomes simulated 20 characters per generation, with the winning strategies passed along through pickle implementation.
